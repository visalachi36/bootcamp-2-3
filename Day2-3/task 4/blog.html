ABOUT ROHIT SHARMA

Rohit Sharma (born 30 April 1987) is an Indian cricketer and the captain of the national cricket team in Test and ODI formats. He is widely regarded as one of the best opening batters of all time. He is also known for hitting sixes. He is a right-handed batsman who plays for Mumbai Indians in the Indian Premier League and for Mumbai in domestic cricket. Rohit previously captained India in all three formats. After leading India to victory at the 2024 Men's T20 World Cup, he announced his retirement from T20Is.

He holds several batting records, including the most runs in T20 Internationals, the most sixes in international cricket, the most double centuries in ODI cricket (3), the most centuries at Cricket World Cups (7), and the joint most hundreds in Twenty20 Internationals. He is the first player to score 5 T20I centuries and holds the record for the fastest T20I century in 35 balls. He also holds the world record for the highest individual score (264) in a One Day International (ODI) match and is the only player to have scored three double centuries in ODIs. Additionally, he holds the record for scoring the most hundreds (five) in a single Cricket World Cup, for which he won the ICC Men's ODI Cricketer of the Year award in 2019. He is the only player to win 50 matches as captain in T20Is.

He has received two national honors: the Arjuna Award in 2015 and the prestigious Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna Award in 2020 by the Government of India. Under his captaincy, India won the 2018 Asia Cup and the 2023 Asia Cup, the seventh and eighth times the country won the title, both in ODI format, as well as the 2018 Nidahas Trophy, their second overall and first in the T20I format.
